💣 Gaza–Israel Ceasefire Crumbles

By Zawadi Intel Editorial Desk — October 26, 2025

In a devastating escalation, Israeli airstrikes leveled residential blocks in Khan Younis and Jabalia, killing dozens and reigniting fears of a full-scale ground invasion. The collapse of ceasefire negotiations has plunged Gaza deeper into humanitarian crisis.

The image above, captured at dawn, shows a father carrying his injured daughter through rubble-strewn streets. Behind them, a collapsed apartment complex smolders as emergency crews search for survivors. The child’s face is streaked with ash, her arm wrapped in a bloodied cloth.

International mediators have called for an immediate halt to hostilities. President Trump proposed a multinational peacekeeping force, but Israel rejected Turkish participation, citing security concerns. The UN warns of a catastrophe as food, water, and medical supplies dwindle.

Military analysts say the strikes aim to dismantle Hamas’s tunnel networks and command centers. Israeli officials claim the operation is “surgical,” while Palestinian sources report widespread civilian casualties and infrastructure collapse.

With over 68,000 dead, Gaza has become a symbol of survival and resistance. Aid workers describe it as “a city of ghosts,” where families sleep in bombed-out schools and children draw tanks in trauma clinics. The war now wages across memory, infrastructure, and identity.

As the war enters its third year, the battlefield is no longer confined to trenches and towns — it now includes the economic arteries that fuel conflict. This strike marks a turning point in Ukraine’s strategy: precision, disruption, and psychological warfare.

🔥 Sudan’s Last Stronghold Falls

By Zawadi Intel Editorial Desk — October 25, 2025

In a grim milestone for Sudan’s civil war, the Rapid Support Forces (RSF) have seized El-Fasher, the final Sudanese Armed Forces (SAF) stronghold in Darfur. The fall of the city marks a symbolic collapse of state control and deepens fears of ethnic cleansing.

The image above shows RSF fighters patrolling a scorched street near the city’s central hospital. Smoke rises from a bombed maternity ward as civilians flee with children and bundles of belongings. UN officials warn of “mass atrocities” and demand immediate humanitarian access.

Over 250,000 civilians remain trapped in El-Fasher, with food and water running out. Hospitals have been bombed, and aid convoys blocked. The UN Security Council held an emergency session, but no resolution was passed. Regional powers remain divided on intervention.

Military analysts say the RSF’s strategy is to consolidate control over Darfur’s trade routes and mineral zones. The SAF retreat signals a shift toward militia-led governance, with tribal alliances replacing national institutions.

As Sudan’s war enters its second year, the battlefield now includes identity, survival, and memory. Families bury loved ones in courtyards, children draw tanks in refugee camps, and elders speak of Darfur as “a land swallowed by fire.”
