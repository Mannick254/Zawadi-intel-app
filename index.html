🇺🇦 Ukraine Strikes Russian Oil Sites

By Zawadi Intel Editorial Desk — October 22, 2025

In one of the most daring operations of the war, Ukrainian forces launched a series of drone strikes targeting Russian oil infrastructure deep inside enemy territory. The attack ignited massive fires across multiple refineries, sending plumes of black smoke into the night sky and triggering emergency responses from Russian firefighters.

The image above, captured moments after the strike, shows two responders battling flames at a refinery near Belgorod. One firefighter, clad in yellow gear marked “АЗДУМА,” directs a jet of water toward a burning storage tank. The other, in black protective gear, grips the hose with grim determination as flames engulf the industrial complex behind them.

U.S. officials have called for restraint, while former President Trump urged a “freeze on the front lines” to prevent further escalation. NATO surveillance flights have intensified along the eastern corridor, and diplomatic channels remain tense as Moscow vows retaliation.

Military analysts say the strikes were aimed at crippling Russia’s fuel supply chain ahead of winter. Ukrainian officials have not commented publicly, but sources close to Kyiv suggest the operation was coordinated with Western intelligence.

As the war enters its third year, the battlefield is no longer confined to trenches and towns — it now includes the economic arteries that fuel conflict. This strike marks a turning point in Ukraine’s strategy: precision, disruption, and psychological warfare.
