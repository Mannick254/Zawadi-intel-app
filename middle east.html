Gaza: Genocide Allegations and Global Response

International lawyers and UN rapporteurs have accused Israel of committing acts that may constitute genocide in Gaza. The term gained traction after mass civilian casualties.

Israel denies the allegations, calling them politically motivated. It argues that Hamas uses civilians as shields, complicating military operations.

South Africa and Turkey filed motions at the International Criminal Court (ICC), demanding investigations into war crimes and ethnic cleansing.

Global protests erupted in London, Nairobi, and Jakarta, with demonstrators calling for sanctions and arms embargoes against Israel.

Meanwhile, Israeli officials maintain that their actions are lawful under Article 51 of the UN Charter, citing self-defense against terrorism.

The ICC has yet to issue formal indictments, but the debate over genocide continues to polarize global opinion and diplomatic alliances.
