    Kenya Backs Regional Law to Eliminate FGM in East Africa

    By Zawadi Intel Staff | October 26, 2025

    Kenya has taken a leading role in advocating for a unified East African Community (EAC) law to eradicate female genital mutilation (FGM), a harmful practice affecting millions of women and girls.

    The proposed bill seeks to harmonize penalties across member states, ensuring that offenders cannot escape justice by crossing borders.

    FGM remains prevalent in parts of East Africa despite national bans, with cross-border practices undermining enforcement efforts.

    Community education programs are being scaled up to challenge cultural norms that perpetuate FGM.

    International organizations, including the UN, are supporting the EAC with technical expertise and funding.

    Legal experts argue that harmonized laws will strengthen prosecution and reduce loopholes exploited by perpetrators.

    Grassroots campaigns highlight the importance of involving men and boys in ending FGM traditions.

    Regional leaders believe the law could set a precedent for other African blocs to adopt similar frameworks.

    Challenges remain, including resistance from communities where FGM is deeply entrenched culturally.

    Despite obstacles, the bill represents a significant step toward gender equality and safeguarding future generations.
