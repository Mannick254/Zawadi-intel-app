    Asia floods leave 1,300 dead, billions in damages

    By Zawadi Intel Staff | Dec 1, 2025

    Catastrophic cyclones devastate Sri Lanka, Indonesia, and Thailand, displacing millions. The recent floods have caused widespread destruction, with homes, infrastructure, and livelihoods severely impacted across the affected regions.

    Authorities in Sri Lanka report over 500 fatalities, while Indonesia and Thailand face similar tragedies. The economic toll is estimated in billions, straining recovery efforts and international aid.

    Climate experts attribute the severity to changing weather patterns and inadequate preparedness. Communities are calling for better disaster management and sustainable development practices.

    Humanitarian organizations are mobilizing resources to provide relief, including food, shelter, and medical assistance. Long-term recovery plans are being discussed to rebuild resilient communities.

    This disaster underscores the urgent need for global action on climate change, as vulnerable regions continue to bear the brunt of extreme weather events.
