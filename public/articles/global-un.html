The summit marks a pivotal moment in global environmental diplomacy, with leaders gathering to address the escalating climate crisis, biodiversity loss, and pollution.

    Kenya, as the host nation, emphasized the urgency of inclusive and equitable environmental governance. President Ruto welcomed delegates with a call to action, urging nations to move beyond rhetoric and commit to tangible solutions that protect both people and planet.

    Green finance has emerged as a key theme, with calls for reforming global financial systems to prioritize climate resilience. Delegates are discussing debt-for-nature swaps, climate bonds, and equitable access to adaptation funding for vulnerable nations.

    Youth and civil society voices are prominent at UNEA-7, with activists demanding accountability and intergenerational justice. Side events feature grassroots campaigns, indigenous perspectives, and art installations that highlight environmental struggles.
