    WMO warns of rising flood risks across Asia

    By Zawadi Intel Staff | Dec 1, 2025

    Climate Change and Rainfall

    UN agency cautions that climate change is intensifying rainfall and disasters in Southeast Asia. The World Meteorological Organization highlights the increasing frequency and severity of flooding due to global warming.

    Regional Vulnerabilities

    Experts warn that vulnerable regions are facing heightened risks, with potential for more devastating events in the coming years. Adaptation strategies are urgently needed to mitigate impacts.

    Human Factors

    The report emphasizes the role of deforestation and urbanization in exacerbating flood risks. Governments are urged to invest in early warning systems and sustainable land management.

    Global Cooperation

    International cooperation is key to addressing this global challenge, with calls for enhanced data sharing and technology transfer to at-risk countries.

    Future Preparedness

    As climate patterns shift, proactive measures can help communities prepare and respond effectively to future disasters.
