India Launches Inclusive AI Mission for the Global South

By Zawadi Intel Staff | Dec 13, 2025

Indiaâ€™s Vision for Equitable AI

India has launched its "Inclusive AI Mission for the Global South," aiming to democratize access to artificial intelligence. Announced at the Global Digital Summit in New Delhi, the mission positions India as a leader for developing nations in shaping the digital future.

Challenging Digital Colonialism

The initiative counters the dominance of AI by a few nations in the Global North, raising concerns about digital colonialism. India advocates for open-source AI, knowledge sharing, and capacity building to empower nations across Africa, Latin America, and Southeast Asia.

Strategic Pillars of the Mission

The mission rests on three pillars: open-source AI models tailored to local challenges, a network of AI Centers of Excellence, and a Global South AI Fund to support startups tackling climate change, poverty, and inequality.

Building a Collaborative Ecosystem

Indian universities and research institutions will partner with counterparts worldwide to co-develop AI curricula, host hackathons, and facilitate student exchanges. These collaborations aim to nurture talent rooted in local realities and foster sustainable innovation.

Reception Across the Global South

Leaders from Kenya, Brazil, and Indonesia have welcomed the initiative, viewing it as a chance to leapfrog traditional development pathways. Civil society groups praise its emphasis on open-source tools and ethical AI.

Criticism and Challenges

Critics question Indiaâ€™s record on digital rights and data privacy, while others doubt the scale and sustainability of the mission. Concerns include infrastructure limitations and the need for long-term funding.

Geopolitical Implications

The mission is a geopolitical statement positioning India as a third force in the global tech race, challenging the dominance of the US and China. It signals a shift toward a multipolar digital future rooted in ethical innovation.

Conclusion

If successful, Indiaâ€™s Inclusive AI Mission could redefine global technology leadership and accelerate AI adoption across the Global South. The world is watching to see whether this bold vision can become reality.
