    UNEA-7 Opens in Nairobi with Urgent Climate Agenda

    By Zawadi Intel Staff | Nov 30, 2025

    The Seventh Session of the United Nations Environment Assembly (UNEA-7) has opened in Nairobi, bringing together over 170 countries to tackle pressing environmental issues. The summit focuses on climate change, biodiversity loss, and pollution, with urgent calls for global action.

    Delegates are debating innovative solutions to combat the triple planetary crisis: climate disruption, nature and biodiversity loss, and pollution and waste. Kenya, as the host, emphasizes the need for equitable and inclusive approaches to environmental governance.

    Key topics include sustainable development, green finance, and the role of technology in environmental protection. Experts warn that without immediate action, the planet faces irreversible damage, affecting millions worldwide.

    The assembly aims to strengthen international cooperation and commitments to the Paris Agreement and other environmental accords, fostering a sustainable future for all.
